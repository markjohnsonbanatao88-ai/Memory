# Pandora Review Queue UX

## Purpose

Keep candidate review, pruning review, and admin memory screens clear, explainable, and operator-friendly.

## UX rules

- Do not dump everything on one page without grouping.
- Group by urgency, namespace, category, and risk.
- Show why an item needs review.
- Show score labels in plain language, not only raw math.
- Make approve, dismiss, keep, supersede, and review actions obvious.
- Do not hide the reason a memory is stale, unsafe, duplicate, or superseded.
- Keep dangerous actions visually distinct and confirmation-gated.
- Prefer progressive disclosure for raw details.

## Required labels

Use labels like:

- High usefulness
- Medium usefulness
- Low usefulness
- Aging
- Possibly superseded
- Needs review
- Unsafe candidate
- Duplicate candidate

## Forbidden

- Do not bury high-risk candidates below low-value noise.
- Do not make destructive or irreversible actions one-click.
- Do not expose raw secret-like text in review cards.

## Completion standard

Report what the operator can understand and decide within 30 seconds, and what still needs better grouping or explanation.
